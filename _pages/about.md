# About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science in the [Information and Computer Sciences Department](https://www.ics.hawaii.edu/) at the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa, advised by [Prof. Haopeng Zhang](https://hpzhang94.github.io/). I received my M.Eng. in Computer Science from Nanyang Technological University, where I was advised by [Prof. Siu Cheung Hui](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=d4ZYx6gAAAAJ), and my B.Sc. in Mathematics and Applied Mathematics from Hefei University of Technology.

My research focuses on reliable and explainable AI systems. I study how structural priors can improve reasoning, robustness, and controllability in large language models, multimodal systems, and embodied agents. My interests include LLM reasoning, long-context understanding, model attribution, multimodal and agentic AI, and systematic evaluation.

I am actively seeking research internship or visiting student opportunities in LLM algorithms, multimodal and agentic AI, and reliable AI systems.

# Current Research

### StrucVLA: Structural Priors for Explainable Vision-Language-Action Policies

- Developing structured high-level task states for embodied policies, decomposing implicit VLA understanding into scene, task, interaction, and progress variables.
- Building a Unified State Binding pipeline that uses multimodal LLMs as offline semantic teachers to generate structured state supervision with verifier- and rule-based quality control.
- Evaluating high-level state representations through diagnosis, ablations, corrupted-state tests, and counterfactual state control.

### SumFingerprint: Reliability and Fragility of LLM Summary Fingerprints

- Studying whether LLM-generated summaries contain model-specific stylistic fingerprints that remain reliable under rewriting and transformation attacks.
- Building controlled attribution benchmarks and interpretable feature analyses, and examining fingerprint fragility and targeted misattribution risks.
